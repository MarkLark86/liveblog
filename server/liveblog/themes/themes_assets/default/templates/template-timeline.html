Homage to "The Sound of Music"

As every time, the Life Ball is subject to a theme that inspires guests to their outfits and stage decoration as theme of the opening show. This year, the musical film "The Sound of Music" from 1965 serves as the template. The Broadway premiere was in 1959, and production in London started in 1961.

ORF.at/Roland Winkler

The film is set in the 1930s and is about the singing family choir Trapp. The historic Trapp family escaped from the National Socialists in 1938 via Switzerland to the United States, where it gained great fame. Ball guests from English-speaking countries will probably be able to sing along better than Austrian songs. In this country, the film is almost unknown, you know that it exists, but hardly anyone has seen him.

Postcard kitsch as a backdrop

For the second time, set designer Amra Bergman was responsible for the setting, which this year, how could it be otherwise, offers a lot of alpine scenery: a puzzle of meadows and mountains, with edelweiss, an oversized cuckoo clock and folk couple in the midst of butterflies and deer.

ORF.at/Roland Winkler
